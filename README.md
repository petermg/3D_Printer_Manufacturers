# AB 2047 3D Printing Outreach Tool

A free, static outreach tool that helps 3D printing users respectfully contact printer manufacturers about California AB 2047.

**Live site:** https://petermg.github.io/3D_Printer_Manufacturers/

## What this is

California AB 2047 raises serious questions for the 3D printing community, including printer-level blocking technology, false positives, slicer compatibility, open workflows, firmware control, privacy, cost, and whether manufacturers may restrict or stop selling certain printers in California.

This project gives ordinary 3D printing users a simple way to ask manufacturers to speak up about those technical and practical concerns.

The tool lets users:

- Select one or many 3D printing companies.
- Personalize a respectful message.
- Generate individual email drafts for each company.
- Open drafts in a normal email app or Gmail.
- Use official contact pages when no direct public email is available.
- Copy a short share message for Reddit, Discord, Facebook groups, forums, and other communities.

## What this is not

This is **not** an automated email sender, spam tool, petition platform, tracking system, or data-collection site.

The site does not send emails by itself. It does not collect visitor information. It does not have a backend. Emails are generated as drafts in the visitor’s own email app or Gmail, and the visitor decides what to send.

## How to use it

1. Open the live site.
2. Add your name, location, printer/customer note, and optional personal note.
3. Choose an email template.
4. Select the companies you use or care about.
5. Generate drafts.
6. Review each draft, personalize it if needed, and send it from your own email account.

For best results, contact companies you genuinely use, buy from, recommend, or care about. Respectful, personal messages are more likely to be read than copy-paste blasts.

## Why individual emails?

Individual emails are usually more effective than a giant CC/BCC message because they are more direct, less likely to be filtered as spam, and less likely to be dismissed as automated bulk outreach.

This tool can generate drafts for multiple selected companies, but it prepares them as separate messages.

## Contact directory

The included contact directory contains public-facing contact information for major 3D printing companies, including consumer, prosumer, resin, industrial, and professional additive-manufacturing companies.

Where available, the directory includes media, marketing, sales, business, partner, support, privacy/legal, investor, and general contact paths. Some companies only publish contact forms or regional support pages instead of direct public emails.

Contact information changes. Please verify important contacts before relying on them for major outreach.

## Contributing updates

Contact corrections are welcome. Helpful updates include:

- Better media or marketing contacts.
- Confirmed company responses with suggested departments.
- Broken or outdated email addresses.
- Missing companies.
- Incorrect company segments.
- Better source links.

When suggesting a contact update, include where the information came from, such as an official contact page, company response, press page, support page, or public company document.

## Tone and conduct

Use this tool for respectful civic outreach.

Do not threaten, harass, flood inboxes, impersonate anyone, or send deceptive messages. The goal is to encourage companies to publicly evaluate AB 2047 and explain its likely impact on legitimate 3D printing users, educators, makerspaces, repair users, engineers, artists, hobbyists, and small businesses.
